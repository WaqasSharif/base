Helping You Honor Those Who Have Passed

Over the years, cremation has become an increasingly popular option. Offering flexibility on when and where the service can be held, this choice is convenient and customizable. You can have a traditional funeral service before cremation takes place or a memorial service at any time with or without the ashes present. If the ashes are present, you can have the urn buried in a grave or columbarium where we can hold a funeral service. You can also keep your loved ones' remains if you so choose. This way, the ashes can be kept or scattered in the loving memory of your friend or family member who has passed. No matter what you choose, we’re here to offer you a beautiful and meaningful ceremony.

Planning a Cremation Service

Cremation is not only a more budget-friendly than traditional internment, but it also allows for flexibility in memorial planning. We understand that many people still want to have proper memorial service and burial even when the person’s physical body is no longer with us. We provide personalized options that can meet all your ceremonial requirements. When you rely on us for cremation services, you can expect the following:

  • The choice between an urn or casket: We carry a wide range of wood, marble, ceramic, and bronze urns for your choosing. We even offer biodegradable urns in a variety of styles to accommodate water burials and green cemeteries since they are designed to break down naturally over time. If you prefer a traditional casket, we offer specially made caskets constructed specifically for cremation.
  • Full say in planning the memorial: Do you prefer a private ceremony? Maybe you need the memorial to be held immediately? Would you prefer to have the service at your house, a church, or our funeral home? Will the funeral be held with or without the urn present? We’ll offer our professional guidance through these difficult decisions so you can pay your respects in a customized, meaningful way.
  • Deciding what will be done with the ashes: After the memorial, you can choose to bury the ashes in an urn vault or columbarium, have the ashes scattered in a meaningful location, or have a loved one take the ashes home.
